So what does it actually take to deliver on this **ambition** — thousands of agents wielding thousands of tools, with the balance between human effort, AI labor, and traditional computation shifting smoothly across every workflow, for every decision?  
  
It takes more than a data lake. More than an ML platform. More than an application builder. It takes a unified architecture purpose-built for this kind of composability — and that architecture is organized around one central concept: the **Ontology**.  
  
The Ontology is the layer at the heart of the platform. Ontology is the shared world where both humans and AI operate. Around the Ontology, the platform provides the full set of services needed to make this work at enterprise scale.

![](https://everpath-course-content.s3-accelerate.amazonaws.com/instructor%2F6rv9eqj974u5ivyxlj7ndbkii%2Fpublic%2F1777629205%2FHigh+Level+Arch+1.1777629204744.jpg)

Taken together, this is what we call **AI Infrastructure** — the layer between raw AI capabilities and actual enterprise outcomes. Frontier models are increasingly powerful, but **a model alone can't understand your business, respect your governance policies, integrate with your existing systems, or take actions that affect real-world operations**. AI Infrastructure is what bridges that gap. And at its center, the Ontology is what makes the whole thing composable.

![](https://everpath-course-content.s3-accelerate.amazonaws.com/instructor%2F6rv9eqj974u5ivyxlj7ndbkii%2Fpublic%2F1777629555%2FHigh+Level+Arch+2.1777629554762.jpg)

The rest of this course walks through each of these layers in detail — starting with the Ontology at the center, then exploring what feeds into it and what's built on top of it.
