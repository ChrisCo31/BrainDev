The first thing most organizations build on top of the Ontology is visibility. Analytics and workflows are where the Ontology becomes tangible — where people see their business reflected in data and start making decisions based on it.

![](https://everpath-course-content.s3-accelerate.amazonaws.com/instructor%2F6rv9eqj974u5ivyxlj7ndbkii%2Fpublic%2F1777641553%2FA%26W+cropped.1777641553743.png)

**Workflows** connect people and processes across business functions. Rather than operating in silos, workflows orchestrate activity across the Ontology. A single workflow might span supplier monitoring, inventory allocation, production scheduling, and customer delivery, connecting the people responsible for each step and surfacing the information they need at each stage.  
  
**Real-time analytics and dashboards** provide ongoing visibility into operations. Because they're built on the Ontology, these aren't static reports generated from a data warehouse — they reflect the live state of your business objects, complete with the logic and computed properties that make the data meaningful. Foundry provides a comprehensive workbench for building dashboards, reports, and visualizations, supporting low-code, no-code, and pro-code approaches.  
  
**The workflow-analytics connection** is important. In traditional BI, analytics are an end in themselves — you produce a report and hope someone acts on it. In Foundry, analytics are a byproduct of the workflows you're building. The insights emerge from the operational processes, and decisions can be acted on immediately within the same environment.
