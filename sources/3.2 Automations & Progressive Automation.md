Automations in Foundry combine generative AI with the full context of the Ontology to create intelligent, governed workflows that can reason about your business and act on it.

![](https://everpath-course-content.s3-accelerate.amazonaws.com/instructor%2F6rv9eqj974u5ivyxlj7ndbkii%2Fpublic%2F1777643766%2FA%26PA+cropped.1777643765887.png)

**Reasoning with Business Logic.** One of the most powerful patterns in AIP is the ability for LLMs to call deterministic models as part of their reasoning chain. An LLM might interpret an unstructured maintenance report, and then call a deterministic failure-prediction model to assess the risk score — combining the LLM's ability to parse natural language with the precision of a deterministic tool. The LLM has access to both the crucial data and the business logic that are modeled in the Ontology. This is a critical distinction: the LLM isn't operating alone. It's orchestrating across multiple sources of logic — some AI-powered, some rule-based, some model-driven, some based on human intervention — all unified through the Ontology.  
  
**Orchestrating Complex Actions.** Once you've captured a decision-making process in the Ontology, you can enhance it with agent orchestration. The agent isn't a standalone tool sitting outside your workflow. It's embedded directly into the business process, reasoning with the same logic and taking actions through the same governed channels that a human operator would use.  
  
**AIP Evals: Building Confidence for Agentic Workflows.** Evals are the cornerstone of effective agentic workflows — because when agents reason with your Ontology, take actions in your systems, and operate inside your business processes, observability isn't optional. It's the foundation that makes everything else trustworthy. The platform provides a structured evaluation environment for testing AI-powered functions and agents, providing full observability over all workflows and automations.
