This brings us to the core insight behind how the platform is actually used: the question is not human OR AI, but **human AND AI**, in the right composition. Every workflow involves a mix of human effort, AI labor, and traditional computation.  
  
Tying it all together: the Ontology is what makes all of this possible. It's the shared world where humans see their business in familiar terms, AI agents access the context they need to reason effectively, every action is governed and auditable, and the composition of human and AI labor can shift over time without breaking the underlying architecture.  
  
This is what composability means in practice. Because the Ontology provides a shared world where humans and AI can both operate — seeing the same data, using the same logic, taking actions through the same governed framework — you can adjust the mix fluidly.
