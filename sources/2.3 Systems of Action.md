Data and logic are only valuable if they lead to action. Systems of Action represent the ability to write decisions back into the enterprise systems that run your operations.  
  
If the you notice in the Ontology that Warehouse A is running low on a critical component and a forecasting model predicts that demand will spike next week, what do you do? You might create a stock transfer order, accelerate next shipment, or adjust the production schedule. Each of these is an action, and each one needs to land in the right enterprise system.  
  
Foundry connects to all your systems of action. These actions are governed — permissions control who (or what) can execute them — and they're auditable, so you always know what happened and why.
