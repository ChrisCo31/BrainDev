To understand why Foundry and AIP are architected the way they are, it helps to understand the problem they were built to solve.  
  
Most organizations manage data like an assembly line: data producers collect it, integration teams combine it, transformation teams convert it, reporting teams generate dashboards, and decision-makers consume the results. This linear flow works — up to a point. But it leaves critical gaps. Learnings from data processing are rarely captured back into the system. Decisions made from insights aren't always materialized into action — someone sees a dashboard, realizes a shipment should be rerouted, but has to swivel-chair into a completely different system to actually make it happen. And there's often no effective feedback loop between the front-line operators who see what's really going on and the analytics teams who produce the reports.  
  
Foundry and AIP are built to close these gaps: not just a data platform, but a decision-centric operating system where data flows in, decisions emerge, actions are taken, and the results feed back to improve future decisions.
