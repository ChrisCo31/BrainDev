To make decisions in any business, you need three things: the data (what IS), the logic (how to THINK about it), and the actions (what you can DO). The Ontology brings all three together, enabling human and AI teams to leverage them.  
  
When you build on the Ontology, you create a **shared foundation** that serves both humans and AI simultaneously:

- **Data** — all modalities of data (structured, unstructured, time series, geospatial) are unified into business objects that both people and AI agents can understand and query.
- **Logic** — business rules, ML models, optimizers, and all forms of traditional compute are modeled as part of the Ontology, so both human operators and AI agents can call upon the same reasoning tools.
- **Action** — the actions you can take to affect the real world (creating an order in SAP, adjusting a schedule, flagging an issue) are modeled in the Ontology with full governance guardrails, available to both humans and AI.

Finally, it is important to establish a **common security and governance framework** — this goes beyond simple role-based access controls to include purpose-based controls, data markings, classifications, and comprehensive audit capabilities that span both human and AI teams.  
  
This is what enables composability. Because humans and AI are working in the same shared world — seeing the same data, reasoning with the same logic, taking actions through the same governed channels — you can smoothly dial up and down the distribution of labor between them.
