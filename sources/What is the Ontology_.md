If you remember one thing from this course, let it be this: the Ontology is the centerpiece of the entire platform.  
  
The Ontology is the nouns and verbs that make up your business. In a manufacturing context, it's plants and warehouses, suppliers and customers, shipments and orders. In healthcare, it's patients and providers, appointments and prescriptions, facilities and equipment. Whatever your domain, the Ontology models how your business actually operates — not how your IT systems need it to be structured so they can function, but how the real world actually works.  
  
This is a semantic layer that bridges the gap between raw data from source systems and the business language that people use every day. It translates complex, multi-system data into familiar business terms, making the data accessible and actionable for everyone — from the data engineer who built the pipeline to the plant manager who needs to make a staffing decision in the next ten minutes.
