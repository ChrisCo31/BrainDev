Foundry connects to the full breadth of enterprise data — regardless of where it lives, what format it's in, or how old the system is.

![](https://everpath-course-content.s3-accelerate.amazonaws.com/instructor%2F6rv9eqj974u5ivyxlj7ndbkii%2Fpublic%2F1777631884%2FData+Sources+Cropped.1777631884024.png)

**Types of data sources** span the full spectrum of what enterprises generate: transactional data from ERPs and CRMs, IoT and sensor data from connected equipment, geospatial data from fleet tracking and logistics, unstructured data like documents, images, and maintenance logs, relational data from databases, and much more.  
  
**Native connectors** provide out-of-the-box connectivity to over 300 enterprise systems. Whether you're pulling from SAP, Salesforce, ServiceNow, or a homegrown legacy system, Foundry has a connector for it. For custom or unusual sources, the platform provides frameworks for building your own connections.  
  
**The Multimodal Data Plane (MMDP) in practice** is where the "any storage" promise becomes concrete. Through Virtual Tables, Foundry can work with data that lives in Snowflake, Databricks, BigQuery, or other platforms — without copying it. The data stays where it is. Foundry accesses it in place, through zero-copy federation. When transformations need to happen, pushdown compute sends the computation to where the data already lives, rather than moving the data to where the compute is. And all of this works with open data standards like Apache Iceberg, Parquet, and CSV, alongside standard interfaces like REST, JDBC, and S3-compatible APIs. To read more about Multimodal Data Plane, refer to [these public docs](https://www.palantir.com/interoperability/).

The critical insight here is that Foundry isn't asking you to abandon your data lake or warehouse — it's asking you to connect it. The Ontology layer above turns that connected data into context, and context is what makes data operational.
